What it does

Find gold before others do.

Netherlayer learns the geological, structural, and hydrological signature of every known gold occurrence in Queensland — then applies that pattern to every mapped geological unit to identify locations with the same signature but no recorded gold history. The output is a ranked, explainable probability surface for the entire state.

🏔️

1:100k Solid Geology

Detailed lithological units from the Queensland geological survey — rock type, geological features, structural setting.

⚡

Structural Geology

Fault lines, dykes, veins and intrusion contacts from the state structural dataset — key fluid pathways for gold transport.

💧

Hydrological Network

Named watercourses plus OSM waterways — stream proximity concentrates alluvial gold and reveals catchment signatures.

🗺️

Road Network (OSM)

Full QLD road network — 472,000+ road segments used to score exploration accessibility and estimate prospecting history.

Training Pipeline

Five stages from raw data to actionable prospect.

Each step is a standalone Python script, reproducible from source data.

🏷️
Step 00

Build Gold Labels

Aggregates 21,403 known gold locations from 6 independent sources — KMZ waypoints, QLD Govt MINOCC database, historic and dead mining leases, claims, and MDLs. Deduplicates at a 200m grid.

📐
Step 01

Feature Engineering

Labels each of 209,899 centroid records by KD-tree proximity to gold (1 km radius). Computes 16 spatial features: distances to faults, veins, watercourses, roads, rock class encoding, and structural indicators.

🧠
Step 02

Train Ensemble

Trains Random Forest, XGBoost and LightGBM on 80% of the data with out-of-fold cross-validation. A Logistic Regression meta-learner stacks their probability outputs. Threshold tuned for precision-heavy F₀.₅.

🌐
Step 03

Probability Surface

Applies the stacking ensemble to all 209,899 centroids in batches, generating a full-state gold probability surface. At ≥60% probability, only 862 new (non-database) locations survive — these are the map targets.

🗃️
Step 04+

Enrich & Map

Each prospect is enriched with road distance, opportunity score (probability × remoteness), terrain hint, and plain-English geological explanation. Displayed on an interactive Leaflet map alongside all 21,403 known sites.

Gold Location Sources

Every known gold occurrence in Queensland, unified.

Six independent datasets are merged and deduplicated into a single authoritative training label file.

KMZ Waypoints 7,158

Abandoned gold mines and mineral deposits from AU_QLD_GoldWaypoints.kmz — highest positional precision.

MINOCC Database 1,961

Queensland Government mineral occurrence database (Layer 12, ArcGIS REST service) — same data as Queensland Globe.

Historic Mining Leases 7,327

QLD Govt historic lease point records filtered to AU commodity — large coverage of worked ground.

Dead Mining Leases 3,264

Expired lease polygons — centroid projected via GDA2020/MGA zone 54 for accuracy.

Dead Mining Claims 1,600

Expired small-miner claim polygons — many correspond to individual creek colour finds.

MDLs & Manual 93

Dead mineral development licences plus user-confirmed locations verified in Queensland Globe.

Model Results

Evaluated on a genuine held-out test set.

A stratified 80/20 train/test split ensures all reported metrics reflect real generalisation, not in-sample performance. Threshold tuned for F₀.₅ — precision weighted 4× over recall, because misdirected fieldwork costs more than a missed site.
